The Spread of U.S. Slavery, 1790–1860

How to use this map: Zoom to any county by clicking on it. Clicking on the same county will zoom out. The scales preserve intensity for change over time: in other words, a color represents the same thing for each year on the map. However, the color scales do not preserve intensity from data field to data field: the darkest color for the total population does not represent the same values as for the enslaved population.

The U.S. Census data and shapefiles for these maps comes from Minnesota Population Center, National Historical Geographic Information System, version 2.0 (Minneapolis, MN: University of Minnesota, 2011). For a description of the questions asked on the 1790 to 1860 censuses, see Measuring America: The Decennial Censuses From 1790 to 2000 (U.S Census Bureau, 2002). Bear in mind that the reason we have census statistics on slavery. The federal constitution requires a decennial census in order to apportion Congressional representation and direct taxes. Slaves were counted in census because of the three-fifths compromise, by which an enslaved person counted as three-fifths of a person for representation and taxation. See Below for a description of the fields in the NHGIS database that I have used for each representation on the map.

Explanation of Census Data Used

1790 Census
  • Slave population: "Race/Slave Status: Persons: Non-White: Slave" (AAQ002)
  • Total population: (A00AA1790)
  • Free black population: "Race/Slave Status: Persons: Non-White: Free" (AAQ001)
  • Free white population:"Race/Slave Status: Persons: White" (AAQ003)
  • Slaveholders: sum of "Families by Slaveholding Status by Race: Slaveholding family: White" and "Families by Slaveholding Status by Race: Slaveholding family: Free Colored" (AAE001 and AAE002)
  • Slaves per slaveholder: "Average Number of Slaves per Slaveholding Family" (AAJ001)
  • Percentage slaveholders: calculated from the total of slaveholding families and non-slaveholding families (AAE001, AAE002, AAE003, and AAE004)
1800 Census
  • Slave population: "Nonwhite Population, Except Indians Not Taxed by Slave Status: Slave" (AAY002)
  • Total population: (A00AA1800)
  • Free black population: "Nonwhite Population, Except Indians Not Taxed by Slave Status: Free" (AAY001)
  • Free white population: calculated from the total population minus the slave population and the free black population
1810 Census
  • Slave population: "Nonwhite Population, Except Indians Not Taxed by Slave Status: Slave" (AA7002)
  • Total population: (A00AA1810)
  • Free black population: "Nonwhite Population, Except Indians Not Taxed by Slave Status: Free" (AA7001)
  • Free white population: calculated from the total population minus the slave population and the free black population
1820 Census
  • Slave population: sum of "Nonwhite: slave" male and female columns for "Race/Slave Status by Sex" (ABB003 and ABB004)
  • Total population: (A00AA1820)
  • Free black population: sum of "Nonwhite: free" male and female columns for "Race/Slave Status by Sex" (ABB005 and ABB006)
  • White population: sum of "White" male and female columns for "Race/Slave Status by Sex" (ABB001 and ABB002)
1830 Census
  • Slave population: sum of "Nonwhite: slave" male and female columns for "Race/Slave Status by Sex" (ABO003 and ABO004)
  • Total population: (A00AA1830)
  • Free black population: sum of "Nonwhite: free" male and female columns for "Race/Slave Status by Sex" (ABO005 and ABO006)
  • White population: sum of "White" male and female columns for "Race/Slave Status by Sex" (ABO001 and ABO002)
1840 Census
  • Slave population: "Nonwhite: slave" column for "Race/Slave Status" (ACS003)
  • Total population: (A00AA1840)
  • Free black population: "Nonwhite: free" column for "Race/Slave Status" (ACS002)
  • White population: "White" column for "Race/Slave Status" (ACS001)
